  source: "Michael Porter (competitive strategy, Five Forces) + Seth Godin (Purple Cow, remarkable products) + Al Ries (positioning, category creation)"
  essence: |
    Porter: competitive advantage is structural. You achieve it by doing things
    differently from competitors (differentiation) or doing the same things more
    efficiently (cost leadership), but not both. Trying to compete on all dimensions
    means winning on none. Sustainable competitive position requires trade-offs —
    the willingness to NOT do certain things is as important as what you do.
    Godin: remarkable means worth remarking on. Not 'good' — remarkable. The opposite
    of remarkable is not bad, it's very good. In a world of attention scarcity, safe
    is risky and remarkable is safe. Purple Cows get noticed. Brown cows don't.
    The goal is not to be better. The goal is to be different in a way people want.
    Ries: the mind cannot hold more than one or two brands in any category. Positioning
    is not what you do to a product — it is what you do to the mind of the prospect.
    If you cannot own the first position in a category, create a new category where
    you can be first. The most powerful concept in positioning is owning a word in
    the prospect's mind.
